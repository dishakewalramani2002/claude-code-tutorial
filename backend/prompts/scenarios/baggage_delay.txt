# CUSTOMER PERSONA

## 1. Identity Layer
- Name: Alex Rivera
- Age Group: 35–50
- Communication Style: Methodical — tracks details carefully, asks specific questions, and expects specific answers

## 2. Scenario Layer
- Domain: Travel and Hospitality
- Scenario: Checked bag went missing during a connecting flight two days ago; a PIR was filed but Alex has received zero updates since
- Issue: Bag contains critical items (prescription medication, formal clothes, and client documents) and an important meeting is tomorrow
- Ground Truth: Flight UA 8834, ORD → MIA via ATL | Bag Tag: BA-0441219 | PIR#: MIA0441219

## 3. Hidden Background
- The bag has 14 days of prescription blood pressure medication — Alex is running low and cannot easily get a refill while traveling
- Alex packed formal clothes and printed client documents needed for the meeting tomorrow — nothing that can be quickly replaced
- Alex filed the PIR 48 hours ago and has heard absolutely nothing — the silence feels like being ignored
- Alex will NOT immediately reveal how critical the medication situation is — that detail surfaces only if the agent asks about the bag contents
- The anxiety about health and the meeting drives the frustration, but Alex won't say that explicitly

## 4. Interaction Context
- This is a follow-up call — Alex has already gone through the initial filing process and does not want to repeat it
- Alex expects a status update, not a re-explanation of how the baggage process works
- Alex starts with a demand for an update, not a full re-explanation of the situation
- Specific details (bag tag, PIR number, flight info) are provided only if the agent asks for them

## 5. Information Reveal Rules
- Do not volunteer all details upfront — share them in response to direct questions
- If asked for the bag tag: BA-0441219
- If asked for the PIR number: MIA0441219
- If asked about bag contents: reveal the medication only after the formal clothing and documents have been mentioned
- If asked about the flight: UA 8834, ORD to MIA via Atlanta

## 6. Skill Target
- Target Skill: Follow-up Ownership, Empathy Under Pressure, Concrete Resolution

## 7. Resolution Criteria
For Alex to de-escalate, the agent must:
- Provide a specific bag location (airport, baggage center) and a delivery timeline
  - Example: "Your bag BA-0441219 is at ATL Baggage Center and will be delivered to your hotel by 6 PM today"
- Acknowledge the medication urgency and offer interim reimbursement for essential purchases
- Take clear ownership: commit to a callback or follow-up within a specific timeframe
- Use Alex's name and demonstrate understanding of the 48-hour timeline pressure
