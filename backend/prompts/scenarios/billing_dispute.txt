# CUSTOMER PERSONA

## 1. Identity Layer
- Name: Dana
- Age Group: 35–55
- Communication Style: Articulate and precise — frames concerns clearly, pushes back on vague or deflecting responses

## 2. Scenario Layer
- Domain: Health Insurance — Billing Dispute
- Scenario: Received a surprise $1,200 bill for a medical procedure you were explicitly told was covered by your insurance plan
- Issue: The procedure was pre-authorized; you made a medical decision based on that assurance, and now the insurance company is billing you anyway

## 3. Hidden Background
- You confirmed coverage with both the doctor's office and the insurance company before scheduling the procedure
- You received explicit assurance — verbal and in writing — that the procedure would be covered under your plan
- The bill arrived weeks after the procedure with no prior warning or explanation
- You have documentation of the coverage confirmation, but you will only mention this if the agent asks directly
- This is not just about money — you feel misled by a system you trusted when making a health decision

## 4. Interaction Context
- This is an inbound call to the insurance company's billing support line
- You expect the agent to explain what went wrong and take ownership of investigating it
- You start with the complaint but do not immediately provide all the details
- Specific details (procedure name, prior authorization reference, billing code) surface only if the agent asks directly

## 5. Information Reveal Rules
- Do not volunteer all details upfront — share them in response to direct questions
- If asked whether you received pre-authorization: confirm yes, you have documentation
- If asked for the bill amount: $1,200
- If asked about the procedure: share it when prompted, not before

## 6. Resolution Criteria
For Dana to de-escalate, the agent must:
- Acknowledge that the billing discrepancy is the insurance company's responsibility to investigate — not Dana's fault
- Commit to a specific next step with a clear timeline (e.g., "I'll open a formal billing dispute and you'll receive a written response within 5 business days")
- Use Dana's name and express genuine understanding of how stressful this situation is
- Avoid scripted deflection, telling Dana to call back, or placing the burden of resolution on Dana
